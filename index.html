About

I'm a researcher and engineer working on autonomous AI agents — specifically on how to make them reliably solve complex, long-horizon tasks. I just defended my PhD at Lviv Polytechnic on this exact problem.

My research is not primarily about LLMs. LLMs are a tool. The actual question I care about is: how do you build an agent that doesn't fail halfway through a hard task? That means better architectures for reasoning, better multi-agent coordination, and — increasingly — better infrastructure for measuring and improving agents at scale.

I live between Amsterdam and Lviv, Ukraine.

Experience

2026 – now

Co-Founder

BenchGen

I co-founded BenchGen in early 2026 to build the world's learning infrastructure for AI agents — the continuous loop of data, execution, and intelligence that helps agents evaluate, train, and self-improve.

The core idea: build digital-twin companies inside simulated worlds where agents practice, fail, and learn — turning evaluation into training data. A key focus is RLVR — environments where correctness is objectively verifiable, so agents self-improve without human labeling at every step.

Three stacked layers feeding one compounding data flywheel: a benchmark discovery hub (1000+ public benchmarks), a scalable execution engine for reproducible runs, and an autonomous AI agent that closes the improvement loop. Every run makes the system smarter — that's the whole point.

Research

I study autonomous AI agents capable of reliable long-horizon decision-making. My research focuses on architectures that enable language models to reason, plan, coordinate, and continuously improve while interacting with dynamic environments — and on measuring how well they actually do so.

Contributions

Thought Management System (TMS)

Designed a hierarchical reasoning management framework that enables autonomous agents to maintain, evolve, and optimize reasoning processes across extended execution horizons. Published in Journal of Computational Science (Elsevier, 2026).

LLMAgentNet

Proposed a decentralized network architecture for coordinating autonomous LLM agents during long-horizon tasks, improving scalability over hierarchical multi-agent systems. Demonstrated reliable task decomposition and agent collaboration at scale.

Efficient Agent Communication

Developed an inter-agent communication method using semantic references and execution environment variables, achieving up to 7× lower token consumption without loss of coordination quality — directly reducing inference cost at scale.
