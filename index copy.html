Co-Founder

BenchGen · Full-time

BenchGen is building the world's learning infrastructure for AI agents — a continuous loop of data, execution, and intelligence that helps agents evaluate, train, and self-improve.

Think of it as a flight simulator for AI agents: instead of testing on static benchmarks or isolated prompts, agents operate inside simulated operational environments that replicate real-world business systems — APIs, tools, and multi-step workflows. BenchGen captures decisions, failures, and full task trajectories, turning evaluation into training data for continuous improvement.

The platform is built as three compounding layers:

  • Discovery — the world's hub for benchmarks, RL environments, datasets & tools. 1000+ public benchmarks, open contributions, centralized discoverability
  • Execution — run, train, fine-tune & analyze at scale. Secure scalable infra, reproducible runs, millions of trajectories
  • Intelligence — the BenchGen AI Agent: an autonomous AI engineer that creates tasks & environments, designs rewards & verifiers, detects failures, fine-tunes, and iterates until an agent consistently outperforms a target

Every benchmark, run, and trajectory makes the BenchGen AI Agent smarter — a compounding data flywheel that grows harder to replicate over time. BenchGen is the direct application of my PhD research on autonomous AI agents and long-horizon reasoning.

Research

I study autonomous AI agents capable of reliable long-horizon decision-making. My research focuses on architectures that enable language models to reason, plan, coordinate, and continuously improve while interacting with dynamic environments — and on measuring how well they actually do so.

Research Contributions

Thought Management System (TMS)

Designed a hierarchical reasoning management framework that enables autonomous agents to maintain, evolve, and optimize reasoning processes across extended execution horizons. Published in Journal of Computational Science (Elsevier, 2026).

LLMAgentNet

Proposed a decentralized network architecture for coordinating autonomous LLM agents during long-horizon tasks, improving scalability over hierarchical multi-agent systems. Demonstrated reliable task decomposition and agent collaboration at scale.

Efficient Agent Communication

Developed an inter-agent communication method using semantic references and execution environment variables, achieving up to 7× lower token consumption without loss of coordination quality — directly reducing inference cost at scale.
